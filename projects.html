🛰️ DEVELOPMENT OF A TOOL FOR RELATIVE POSITIONING WITH SMARTPHONE GNSS OBSERVATION

Hacettepe Geomatik • 2025

Problem Purpose Limitations Workflow Design Output Conclusion

Problem Definition

GNSS receivers in smartphones provide lower positional accuracy than geodetic-grade devices due to higher measurement noise, multipath effects and frequent phase breaks.

Purpose

Improve smartphone-based positioning accuracy using DGPS correction data computed from TUSAGA-AKTİF stations; evaluate gains against standard SPP.

Limitations

  • Raw GNSS measurements on Android are limited by SDK/device support.
  • iOS does not expose raw GNSS due to platform policies.
  • Requires internet for real-time corrections.
  • Not usable indoors; GNSS signals unavailable.
  • Multipath can degrade accuracy in urban canyons.

The relative approach with DGPS corrections substantially improves smartphone GNSS accuracy; horizontal ≈ 5 m and vertical ≈ 10 m levels were achievable in tests, outperforming SPP.
